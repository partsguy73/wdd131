About Us

2024 marks the 41st anniversary of the Color Country Chorus, a beloved fixture in the vocal music scene of St. George. The Color Country Chorus graces many events throughout the year. Beyond our annual spring show, we host a cherished Christmas program at the St. George Tabernacle, deliver stirring patriotic music at Memorial Day observances, perform the National Anthem at various sporting events, and make numerous appearances at churches, clubs, and conventions upon request. One of our favorite occasions is Valentine's Day, when quartets from our chorus deliver “Singing Valentines” across the St. George area. These performances bring joy not only to spouses but also to elderly residents and shut-ins, brightening their day with our heartfelt harmonies.

Chris Leonard - Chorus Director

Chris graduated from Dixie State University, with a BS in Music Education and is now the choir director of the Snow Canyon High School. Originally from northern Nevada, he moved to St. George in 2008 where he met his wife and together built a family that includes three boys. Chris grew up with a love of singing and composing music. He’s had the opportunity to sing in many different styles, including jazz, barbershop, opera, art song, and choral. His first taste of complex choral music was during a state choir festival directed by Eric Whitacre, and he has been hooked on the nuance of sound ever since. Currently, Chris is directing the Color Country Chorus and sings in a barbershop quartet and the St. George Chamber singers.
